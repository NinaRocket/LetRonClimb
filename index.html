Meet Ron

Ron is a 74-year-old climber from Auburn, Massachusetts. He has been climbing at Purgatory Chasm for more than three decades, and the chasm has become part of his family’s story.

  • ✓ He climbs with safety gear and checks his ropes carefully.
  • ✓ He has helped visitors who were stuck or in danger.
  • ✓ He alerts park staff about hazards like broken glass or graffiti.
  • ✓ He treats Purgatory Chasm like a place worth protecting.

“A complete end to climbing would not just close a route. For Ron, it would close a chapter of his life.” — The family behind Let Ron Climb

Why Purgatory Chasm matters

Purgatory Chasm is not just another climbing spot. For Ron, it is close to home, familiar, manageable, and deeply meaningful. Other climbing areas may exist nearby, but they are not always practical for an older climber who needs a shorter approach and a setup he knows well.

💚

It keeps him active

Climbing is one of Ron’s main forms of exercise and one of the ways he keeps moving, strong, and connected to the outdoors.

🧗

It is his passion

Ron has loved climbing for decades. Anyone who knows him knows that climbing is woven into who he is.

🌲

He cares for the park

Responsible regulars can be part of a park’s safety and stewardship culture, helping notice hazards and protect the place they love.

A safe path forward

We recently learned that climbing access at Purgatory Chasm may change after this year. Before climbing access ends, we are respectfully asking the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation to consider options that balance safety, stewardship, and tradition.

Annual climbing permits Designated climbing areas Safety gear requirements Ranger check-in process Limited access for experienced climbers Seasonal or time-based restrictions Volunteer cleanup partnership Clear posted safety rules
